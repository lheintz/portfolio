Within electrical engineering, she concentrated in two areas: signals processing and computer engineering. As a part of her computer engineering classes she has completed several integrated projects with microprocessors and arduinos, as well as taken multiple C algorithms courses. She has taken advanced coursework on signal processing and communication theory. In these classes she has completed projects using matlab and designed lab experiments with GNU Radio/Software Defined Radios.

Upon graduation from Purdue University in 2016 she moved to Seattle, Washington, for a job opportunity at Boeing. She nows works in Everett at the manufacturing site of the Boeing twin aisle planes in the Avionics Design Center. She works in Flight Deck Displays, helping to design and test the next generation of Displays and Crew Alerting Systems for the 777X plane.
