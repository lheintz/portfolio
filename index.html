About Lauren

Lauren was born and raised in Cincinnati, OH. She attended Saint Ursula Academy for high school, and was an active martial artist all throughout her childhood and high school.

For college, she left the nest to pursue a B.S. in Electrical Engineering from Purdue University. She graduated With Distinction (GPA>3.85) in 2016 with a B.S. EE, and a Minor In Psychology. Throughout college she was actively involved in on campus organizations such as Honors Engineering Learning Community, Women in Engineering Leadership Team, Old Masters, Boiler Gold Rush, BoilerMake Hackathon Exec Board, and St. Thomas Aquinas Campus Leadership Team.

Within electrical engineering, she concentrated in two areas: signals processing and computer engineering. As a part of her computer engineering classes she has completed several integrated projects with microprocessors and arduinos, as well as taken multiple C algorithms courses. She has taken advanced coursework on signal processing and communication theory. In these classes she has completed projects using matlab and designed lab experiments with GNU Radio/Software Defined Radios.

Upon graduation from Purdue University in 2016 she moved to Seattle, Washington, for a job opportunity at Boeing. She nows works in Everett at the manufacturing site of the Boeing twin aisle planes in the Avionics Design Center. She works in Flight Deck Displays, helping to design and test the next generation of Displays and Crew Alerting Systems for the 777X plane.

Outside of work, she is an avid health and fitness enthusiast. Since moving to Seattle, she has completed her first half marathons, summitted countless peaks, and taken to skiing annd rock climbing. She enjoys the mountains in the summer and the winter, and loves the city life for everything in between. She is also an avid and sometimes impulsive traveler. She's been lucky enough to go to Europe twice over the past two years, as well as domestic visits to see friends and family in Arizona, Califoria, Oregon, Miami, Las Vegas, and Ohio.
